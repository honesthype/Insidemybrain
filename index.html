🌊 Box Breathing
Used by everyone from astronauts to Navy SEALs, box breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system and genuinely reduces cortisol. Especially useful when the ADHD brain is in overdrive.
```
Inhale
4 counts
Hold
4 counts
Exhale
4 counts
Hold
4 counts
Ready
4
Box breathing helps slow a racing brain. Each cycle is 16 seconds. Start with 3 rounds and build from there.
💡 Why it works for ADHD brains
When the ADHD brain is overwhelmed, the body's stress response kicks in and floods the system with cortisol. Box breathing directly counteracts this by stimulating the vagus nerve, which activates the body's rest-and-digest mode. Research shows that just 3 to 5 minutes of controlled breathing can measurably reduce anxiety, improve working memory, and make it easier to start tasks. Think of it as a manual override for your nervous system.
```
